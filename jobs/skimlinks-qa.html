About Skimlinks

We are known for having disrupted the online content monetization space and have become leaders in the industry. We work with thousands of publishers globally and make their lives easier by automating their affiliate marketing activities.

About the Role

Skimlinks is growing fast and we are looking for an experienced QA Engineer / Web Test Engineer to join our team in London. You will be responsible for end-to-end testing and quality assurance for every product and interface across the Skimlinks platform. This testing will be predominantly manual initially but will move to automation in the short-term.

Some of the key responsibilities will be:

  • Testing all aspects of a web service, from back-end databases, scripts and code modules, to front-end design, usability, and interaction design.
  • Testing functionality and usability of all front-end systems including user reporting and admin interfaces and any other user-facing systems we develop.
  • Identify, create and execute functional tests and test scenarios for validating performance against requirements.
  • Develop test data, execute test scripts and analyse results on testing of all aspects of our web application and APIs.
  • Develop, execute and maintain SQL queries to validate completeness, integrity and accuracy of data on the backend.
  • Design and develop automated solutions to increase the efficiency of testing processes.
  • Track, plan and coordinate the resolution of bugs, issues and improvements to ensure defect assignments are resolved within a timely manner.
  • Provide and manage Sprint QA task estimations within a scrum/agile team.
  • Document and communicate project status and complex issues effectively to team members and stakeholders.
  • Create and maintain a virtualized QA environment to ensure the Skimlinks platform continues to operate with all combinations of browsers, operating systems, plug-ins, ISPs, and anti-virus / phishing solutions.
  • Review product specifications to provide feedback based on usability, feasibility, and complexity.
  • Help define and establish new methodologies, processes, and best practices.
